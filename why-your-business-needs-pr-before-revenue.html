Why Your Business Needs PR Before It Needs More Revenue

By Anna Tran · Level Up With Anna · July 15, 2025 · 7 min read

Every business owner I talk to says the same thing: "I'll invest in PR once I hit [insert revenue goal]."

It's the most expensive mistake in business. Not because PR costs a fortune - but because waiting costs you opportunities you'll never even know you missed.

Let me be blunt: if you're running a real business and you don't have a PR strategy, you're leaving authority, trust, and revenue on the table. Every single day.

The Revenue-First Trap

Here's how most entrepreneurs think about growth:

Step 1: Make more money.
Step 2: Spend money on marketing.
Step 3: Maybe do some PR eventually.

The problem? This sequence assumes revenue comes first and reputation follows. In reality, it's the opposite.

"People don't buy from businesses they've never heard of. They buy from businesses they trust. And trust is built through visibility - not sales pitches."

Think about the last time you chose between two service providers. One had been featured in Forbes, had a Google Knowledge Panel, and showed up in multiple search results with authority content. The other had a decent website and some Google reviews.

Who did you trust more? Exactly.

PR doesn't replace your sales process. It makes every part of your sales process work harder.

The 5 Revenue Levers PR Actually Pulls

1. Credibility Shortcut

A single feature in a major publication does what months of content marketing can't - it positions you as a verified expert. Third-party validation is the fastest shortcut to trust. When the media covers you, prospects skip the "Can I trust this person?" phase entirely.

2. SEO Authority You Can't Buy

Backlinks from high-authority media sites are the single most powerful SEO signal. One article in a publication with a Domain Authority of 80+ does more for your Google rankings than 100 blog posts on your own site. And those rankings compound - they keep driving traffic for years.

3. Referral Acceleration

When someone refers your business, the first thing the prospect does is Google you. What they find determines whether that referral converts. PR ensures they find authority, credibility, and expertise - not a blank page or worse, a competitor's content ranking for your name.

4. Premium Pricing Power

Businesses with strong media presence charge 20-40% more than competitors with identical services. Why? Perceived value. "As featured in..." isn't just a logo on your website. It's a signal that you operate at a different level. Clients expect to pay more for recognized experts.

5. Partnership & Opportunity Magnet

Speaking invitations, podcast features, joint ventures, board positions - these don't come from cold emails. They come from visibility. When you show up consistently in media, opportunities find you. The ROI on a single strategic partnership often exceeds years of advertising spend.

The "I'm Not Ready for PR" Myth

Let me address the objections I hear every week:

"I'm not big enough for PR."

You don't need to be a Fortune 500. Media outlets publish stories about entrepreneurs at every stage - from startups to established businesses. What matters is having a compelling angle, not a massive valuation. Some of the best PR stories come from founders solving real problems in unsexy industries.

"I can't afford PR right now."

You can't afford to be invisible. Every month without PR is a month of lost authority, missed inbound leads, and competitors building the credibility you should have. The question isn't whether you can afford PR - it's whether you can afford to keep paying for every customer through ads while your competitors get featured for free.

"I'll just do it myself."

You could also do your own accounting, legal work, and web development. The question is: should you? PR professionals have journalist relationships built over years, know which angles get coverage, and understand the editorial calendar of every relevant publication. DIY PR is possible. Effective DIY PR at scale is extraordinarily rare.

The Hidden Cost of Waiting: Every day your competitors get media coverage and you don't, the gap widens. Search engines reward consistent publishing. Media relationships take months to build. The authority gap between "started PR in year 2" vs "started PR in year 5" compounds like interest - and not in your favor.

What "Good PR" Actually Looks Like in 2025

Forget the old model of press releases and hoping for the best. Modern PR for business owners is a system:

  • Monthly media placements - Consistent features in relevant publications that your audience actually reads
  • Google Knowledge Panel - The gold standard of digital credibility. When you Google someone and see that sidebar panel, the trust factor multiplies
  • Strategic backlink building - Not spammy link farms. Real editorial coverage from real publications that boost your SEO permanently
  • Thought leadership content - Bylined articles, expert quotes, and industry commentary that position you as the go-to voice
  • Crisis buffer - A wall of positive content that protects your reputation when (not if) something goes wrong

The businesses winning right now aren't the ones spending the most on ads. They're the ones who've built such strong authority that customers come to them pre-sold.

The Bottom Line

Revenue without reputation is fragile. One algorithm change, one ad cost increase, one negative review - and your growth stalls.

Reputation without revenue is impossible. It doesn't work that way. The businesses I've watched grow fastest are the ones that understood something simple:

PR isn't a cost center. It's a growth multiplier. And the best time to start building your media presence was yesterday.

Stop waiting for the "right time." The right time was when you started your business. The second best time is right now.

Your competitors aren't waiting. Neither should you.
