PR Strategy

How to Choose the Right PR Package for Your Business

By Anna Tran · March 18, 2026 · 8 min read

Here's a pattern I see constantly: a business owner decides they need PR. They Google "PR agency," find a few options, pick the cheapest one, and then wonder why nothing changed three months later.

Or the opposite. They sign a $15,000/month retainer when they're still figuring out their messaging. They burn cash on placements nobody reads because the foundation isn't there yet.

Both are the same mistake. Mismatching your PR investment to your business stage.

Let me show you how to get this right.

Why "One Size Fits All" PR Doesn't Work

A business doing $500K in revenue has different PR needs than one doing $10M. That should be obvious, but most agencies sell the same package to both.

The $500K business needs visibility. Media placements that build credibility and drive initial trust. They don't need crisis management or quarterly press conferences. They need proof - proof that they're real, legitimate, and worth paying attention to.

The $10M business needs positioning. They already have customers. What they need is market authority, strategic media coverage that positions them as the category leader, and a crisis playbook for when (not if) something goes wrong.

The rule: Your PR strategy should match where your business is today, not where you want it to be tomorrow. Build the foundation first, then scale the investment.

The Four Stages of PR Investment

After working with hundreds of business owners, I've found that PR needs break down into four clear stages. Most agencies won't tell you this because it means selling you less upfront. We'd rather get you results at the right level than overcharge and underdeliver.

Stage 1: Proof of Existence

Best for: Solopreneurs, side hustlers, early-stage businesses under $500K

  • Consistent media publications (3-4 per month)
  • Google presence building - so people find something when they search your name
  • Basic author profiles and backlinks to your site
  • Goal: When a prospect Googles you, they see credibility - not a blank page

Stage 2: Authority Building

Best for: Established businesses $500K-$3M, growing teams, service-based companies

  • Monthly high-quality publications in industry-relevant outlets
  • Quarterly PR campaigns tied to business milestones
  • SEO-driven content strategy pointing back to your offer
  • Goal: Be the name that comes up when people ask "who's good at [your thing]?"

Stage 3: Market Positioning

Best for: Scaling businesses $3M-$10M, multi-location, preparing for investment or exit

  • 1-2 premium publications per month (think industry magazines, podcasts, panels)
  • Quarterly PR pushes + event-based campaigns
  • Google Knowledge Panel development
  • Crisis management playbook (proactive, not reactive)
  • Goal: Own your category in the media landscape

Stage 4: Category Leadership

Best for: Companies $10M+, preparing for acquisition, national/international reach

  • Full PR retainer with dedicated strategist
  • TV and podcast placements
  • Executive thought leadership program
  • Active crisis management and reputation monitoring
  • SEO dominance strategy across all branded and industry terms
  • Goal: When your industry makes news, journalists call you for the quote

The Three Questions That Tell You Your Stage

Still not sure? Answer these honestly:

1. What happens when someone Googles your name?

If the answer is "not much" - you're Stage 1. If your LinkedIn comes up but no media coverage - Stage 2. If you have media hits but they're outdated or in low-authority outlets - Stage 3. If you dominate page one - you're ready for Stage 4.

2. What's your biggest growth constraint right now?

"People don't know I exist" → Stage 1. "I get referrals but can't scale past them" → Stage 2. "I'm competing for bigger deals but losing to better-known competitors" → Stage 3. "I need to be the undisputed leader before we sell/raise" → Stage 4.

3. What would happen to your business if a crisis hit tomorrow?

If you haven't thought about it - that's a red flag at any stage. But if you're above $3M in revenue and don't have a crisis playbook, you're gambling with everything you've built. One bad Glassdoor review, one viral customer complaint, one lawsuit - and your years of work are at risk.

The real cost of wrong-stage PR: Overspend at Stage 1 and you burn cash on premium placements nobody cares about yet. Underspend at Stage 3 and your competitors eat your positioning while you're still DIY-ing blog posts. Match the investment to the stage.

Red Flags When Evaluating PR Agencies

Now that you know your stage, here's how to spot agencies that will waste your money:

  1. They can't explain what you get. Vague deliverables like "media outreach" and "brand building" without specific quantities or outlets = run.
  2. They sell the same package to everyone. If a solopreneur and a $10M company get the same proposal, the agency doesn't understand stages.
  3. No case studies at your level. Ask for results from businesses your size in your industry. If they can't produce them, you're their experiment.
  4. Long contracts with no benchmarks. Six-month lock-ins with no quarterly reviews = they're optimizing for retention, not results.
  5. They never mention crisis management. Any serious PR firm addresses this, at least at Stage 3+. If they only talk about getting you press and never about protecting you, they're incomplete.

What Good PR Actually Costs in 2026

Let's be direct about numbers, because nobody else will be:

  • Stage 1: $37-$200/month. Enough for consistent publications that build your digital footprint. Don't overpay for this.
  • Stage 2: $2,000-$5,000/month. Real strategy, real placements, real results. This is where most growing businesses should be.
  • Stage 3: $5,000-$10,000/month. Premium outlets, crisis prep, Google Knowledge Panel work. The investment that separates contenders from pretenders.
  • Stage 4: $10,000-$20,000+/month. Full-service, dedicated team, enterprise-level media strategy. Only makes sense at $10M+ revenue.

If someone quotes you $10K/month and you're doing $800K in revenue, they're selling you Stage 4 at a Stage 2 business. Politely decline.

The Bottom Line

PR is one of the highest-ROI investments a business owner can make. But only when it's matched to your stage.

Underspend and you stay invisible. Overspend and you bleed cash on authority nobody's ready to receive. The sweet spot is investing at the level your business actually needs right now, with a clear plan to graduate to the next stage as you grow.

Figure out your stage. Invest accordingly. And work with people who understand the difference.
